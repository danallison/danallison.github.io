Q: What do you do?

Q: What's your background?
A: Professionally, I've bounced around a bit. In college, I first studied music at Naropa University in Boulder, Colorado while also working a part-time job doing video production for a non-profit.

Then I dropped out of college, hitchhiked around the country for a while, and eventually moved to Chicago where I took a bunch of comedy improv classes before getting into animation through Flashpoint Academy (now part of Columbia College). I worked in animation for a while making educational videos for a large corporation.

Then I went to a software development bootcamp, moved to Seattle, and started working as a Ruby on Rails and JavaScript developer at a startup. On nights and weekends, I studied mathematics online through Southern New Hampshire University for a while, then took a break from my day job to attend a data science bootcamp.

Today, I work as the founder of Calculist.
